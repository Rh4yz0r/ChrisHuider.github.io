KLM Hand Tracking

Banner image coming soon!

During my internship at the KLM my main research subject was hand tracking in VR. The KLM was very interested in implementing hand tracking into their new cockpit procedure training. I worked on creating and implementing the hand interactions for every button in the cockpit of the Embrear 190. This includes pressing, turning, pulling, different combinations of those and more different interactions.

You can download my entire research paper (Dutch) here.

Most of these interactions were custom made, as a built in solution wasn't available in the Oculus Integration SDK, wasn't precise enough or simply didn't give a satisfactory feeling. They also included hand posing to improve immersion.

While working on this subject I also made a custom finger tracking system to improve accuracy when interacting. Seeing as a cockpit includes a lot of buttons, switches, etc. the accuracy of the hand tracking selection when grabbing had to be very high.

Sadly I cannot go into any technical details, as it's under a Non Disclosure Agreement (NDA).
