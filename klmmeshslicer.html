KLM Mesh Slicer

Banner image coming soon!

During my internship at the KLM I was tasked with making a VR mesh slicer. The objective was to be able to slice objects with a sword. To make this work, I had to do a lot of research into mesh creation, editing and how Unity stores its meshs' data. Luckily I was already somewhat familiair with this subject, due to having experience with 3D modelling, so I quickly got started.

I started out trying to map out every edge and vertex in the mesh using visible rays and dots. I also needed something I would slice the mesh with, so the solution I came up with was to track the movement of the sword, and make a mesh/plane out of it's trajectory. Now that I had a visual representation of where the mesh would need to be cut and I could take a look at which edges and triangles would be intersected. I wrote an algorithm to detect which edges would be intersected and gave them a different color. You can find an example of this in the videos below. Now that I knew where the cut would be, all that was left to do was create new vertices, edges and triangles at the intersections, separate them into two meshes and voila.

Now ofcourse it all went with a little more difficulty, but in hindsight, it's a very interesting but easy to understand principle.
